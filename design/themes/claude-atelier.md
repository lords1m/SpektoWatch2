# Claude Atelier

A warm gallery-print theme drawing from Anthropic's brand identity — deep
ink on cream paper, coral as the single living accent. The palette of a
museum monograph rather than a laboratory instrument.

## Color Palette

- **Cream Paper**: `#F5EFE4` — warm off-white, the ground
- **Soft Cream**: `#EAE3D4` — secondary surface
- **Deep Ink**: `#1C1A16` — primary text, deepest tone
- **Warm Charcoal**: `#3A352D` — body text, chart marks
- **Stone**: `#86807A` — secondary text, quiet labels
- **Bone**: `#C9C0B0` — rules, dividers, tertiary marks
- **Coral**: `#CC785C` — primary accent, signal color
- **Terracotta**: `#A04A38` — saturated accent, peak signal
- **Dusk**: `#6B2820` — deepest accent, climax tone
- **Tan**: `#D4A574` — secondary warm accent

## Spectral Mapping (for chromatic data)

A perceptually graded ramp from cream paper through warm tans into coral
and finally into deep dusk red. Replaces the cool noise-floor-to-fire ramp
of Spektralgrund with a paper-and-ink warmth — silence reads as blank
page, signal reads as deepening ink.

## Typography

- **Display Serif**: Italiana / Gloock / Young Serif — title presence
- **Body Serif (italic)**: Instrument Serif Italic / Lora — captions, marginalia
- **Sans (caps, tracked)**: Instrument Sans / Outfit — labels, headers
- **Monospace**: DM Mono / JetBrains Mono — numerical, calibration

## Visual Character

Warm. Quiet. Held. The cream paper does not vanish into black; it remains
visible at every margin. Marks land on the paper rather than emerging from
the dark. The coral accent is rare and earned — used only where signal
deserves it.

## Best Used For

Monograph-style design objects, museum-quality prints, editorial-grade
identity work, gallery posters, and any artifact that wants to read as
*printed* rather than *projected*.
